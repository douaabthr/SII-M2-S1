FMPSO: fuzzy-dominance based many-objective particle swarm optimization

The performance of multiobjective evolutionary algorithms, based on Pareto-dominance, deteriorates when applied to many-objective problems which are the problems having higher than three objectives. In view of this, various alternative dominance-based approaches have been suggested in the literature. Another major concern in solving these problems is the selection of proper variation operators for exploring the problemâ€™s solution space. This work proposes a new algorithm, fuzzy-dominance based many-objective particle swarm optimization (FMPSO) that addresses these two concerns. It uses the existing SMPSO as base algorithm and replaces the Pareto-dominance technique used in it with fuzzy-dominance as optimization criterion. Additionally a novel archive updation scheme is introduced which provides improved convergence along with the archive refinement scheme which gives enhanced diversity of solutions. We have conducted a number of experiments on the proposed method using standard benchmark problems and have got improved results for FMPSO over other competing approaches.
