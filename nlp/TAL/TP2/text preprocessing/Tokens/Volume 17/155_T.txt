<s>
A
dynamic
tri-population
multi-objective
evolutionary
algorithm
for
constrained
multi-objective
optimization
problems
</s>
<s>
Balancing
between
the
convergence
feasibility
and
diversity
of
the
population
is
the
key
to
solving
constrained
multi-objective
optimization
problems
CMOPs
</s>
<s>
However
the
existing
constrained
multi-objective
optimization
evolutionary
algorithm
CMOEAs
face
challenges
in
converging
to
the
constrained
pareto
front
CPF
with
well-distributed
feasible
solutions
</s>
<s>
To
address
this
issue
this
paper
proposes
a
dynamic
tri-population
multi-objective
evolutionary
algorithm
called
TDPSCMO
</s>
<s>
In
the
initial
phase
the
first
and
second
populations
evolve
dynamically
to
solve
the
original
CMOP
and
the
unconstrained
Multi-Objective
Optimization
Problem
MOP
</s>
<s>
This
dynamic
offspring
generation
emphasizes
convergence
and
feasibility
</s>
<s>
In
the
later
stages
the
allocation
of
computing
resources
to
the
first
population
is
adjusted
based
on
the
variation
in
offspring
numbers
</s>
<s>
Simultaneously
a
third
population
is
introduced
to
enhance
population
diversity
by
treating
Constraint
Violation
degrees
as
an
additional
objective
function
thus
capturing
more
valuable
information
</s>
<s>
The
performance
of
TDPSCMO
is
further
tested
through
57
benchmark
test
problems
and
21
real-world
applications
using
several
state-of-the-art
algorithms
</s>
<s>
The
results
show
the
competitiveness
of
the
proposed
algorithm
when
addressing
CMOPs
.
</s>
