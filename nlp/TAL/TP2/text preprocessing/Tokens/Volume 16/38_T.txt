<s>
Adaptive
deep
Q
learning
network
with
reinforcement
learning
for
crime
prediction
</s>
<s>
Crime
prediction
models
are
very
useful
for
the
police
force
to
prevent
crimes
from
happening
and
to
reduce
the
crime
rate
of
the
city
</s>
<s>
Existing
crime
prediction
models
are
not
efficient
in
handling
the
data
imbalance
and
have
an
overfitting
problem
</s>
<s>
In
this
research
an
adaptive
DRQN
model
is
proposed
to
develop
a
robust
crime
prediction
model
</s>
<s>
The
proposed
adaptive
DRQN
model
includes
the
application
of
GRU
instead
of
LSTM
unit
to
store
the
relevant
features
for
the
effective
classification
of
Sacramento
city
crime
data
</s>
<s>
The
storage
of
relevant
features
for
a
long
time
helps
to
handle
the
data
imbalance
problem
and
irrelevant
features
are
eliminated
to
avoid
overfitting
problems
</s>
<s>
Adaptive
agents
based
on
the
MDP
are
applied
to
adaptively
learn
the
input
data
and
provide
effective
predictions
</s>
<s>
The
reinforcement
learning
method
is
applied
in
the
proposed
adaptive
DRQN
model
to
select
the
optimal
state
value
and
to
identify
the
best
reward
value
</s>
<s>
The
proposed
adaptive
DRQN
model
has
an
MAE
of
36.39
which
is
better
than
the
existing
Recurrent
Q-Learning
model
has
38.82
MAE
.
</s>
